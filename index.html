Reorder · Food

Redesigning Swiggy's reorder flow

↑ 22% repeat orders within 60 days
Problem

Reorder was buried under 3 taps inside order history. Power users (5+ orders/mo) drove 64% of revenue but felt friction every Tuesday night.

Hypothesis

Surfacing last-3 orders on the home screen with one-tap repeat (skip cart, default address, default payment) would lift D7 reorder rate by 15%+.

Experiments

3-cell A/B: control, "Reorder strip" on home, and "Reorder strip + smart bundle suggestion". Ran on 12% traffic for 21 days, segmented by frequency cohort.

Results

Variant B beat control by 22% on repeat orders (p<0.01). Variant C hurt — bundles confused users at the moment of intent. AOV unchanged, time-to-order ↓ 47%.

Learnings

Don't bundle at decision points. Reduce cognitive load when the user already knows what they want. Power users care about speed, not discovery.

Onboarding · Fintech

PhonePe's merchant onboarding

↓ Drop-off from 68% → 31% in 4 months
Problem

Tier-3 merchants were dropping off at GST/PAN entry. Funnel analysis showed 41% bounced at "Business details", another 27% at video-KYC.

Hypothesis

Most kirana owners didn't understand "Business PAN" vs "Personal PAN" — and the Hindi copy was a literal translation, not vernacular.

Experiments

Localized 9 languages with a UX writer, added an inline OCR step (auto-fill from PAN photo), and replaced video-KYC with async DigiLocker pull. Rolled to 3 states first.

Results

Step-1 drop ↓ 58%, KYC drop ↓ 71%. Net activation up 2.3x. CSAT on onboarding rose from 3.1 → 4.4. ~140k incremental merchants in Q3.

Learnings

Vernacular ≠ translation. Replacing synchronous human steps with deterministic data sources is the highest-ROI lever in Bharat onboarding.

PM Toolkit

Frameworks I actually use.

Frameworks are scaffolding, not religion. Here are the six I reach for most often.

🎯

JTBD

Jobs To Be Done. Anchor on the user's underlying goal, not the demographic. Reframes feature ideas as "hire for a job."

Use when: discovery, persona work, repositioning
⚖️

RICE

Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort. Forces a quant case for every roadmap fight. Beats HiPPO every time.

Use when: quarterly planning, roadmap defense
⭐

North Star Framework

One metric that captures product value delivered. Tree it down to input metrics owned by squads.

Use when: org alignment, OKR-setting
⭕

CIRCLES Method

Comprehend, Identify, Report, Cut, List, Evaluate, Summarize. Structured product-design interviews & whiteboards.

Use when: PM interviews, ambiguous design briefs
📝

PRD Writing

Problem → metrics → user stories → out-of-scope → risks. Short, opinionated, link-rich. The PRD is a contract, not a novel.

Use when: kicking off any feature ≥ 2 sprints
🎲

OKRs

Objective + 3 KRs. KRs are outcomes, never outputs. Cascade only one level — beyond that they become theater.

Use when: quarterly cycles, team alignment
