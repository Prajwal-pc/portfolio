About

Technical enough to understand the build, product-minded enough to challenge why it matters.

I am an MCA graduate based in Bengaluru, building toward an APM path with a mix of technical execution and product thinking. My background in Python, Flask, React, and security-focused systems gives me empathy for implementation constraints, while my product work stays centered on user outcomes, root-cause analysis, and success metrics.

I like working on problems that are just ambiguous enough to need structure: a user symptom that looks obvious on the surface, but hides a deeper behavioral, workflow, or incentive issue underneath. My natural instinct is to break that down into discovery, frame the trade-offs, and propose a solution with a metric that tells us if we actually improved the experience.

Technical Depth

I can reason with engineers, understand implementation realities, and frame product decisions with system constraints in mind.

User-First Thinking

I focus on what the user is actually feeling, assuming, or struggling with before jumping to a feature recommendation.

Data-Driven Decisions

I prefer decisions that connect clearly to a measurable outcome, whether that is conversion, retention, or trust in the flow.

Flagship Research

Personal research works on 10 Bengaluru startups, each written like a compact PM teardown.

This section is meant to read like actual research work, not startup admiration. For each company, I focus on the operating constraint behind the growth story: where activation leaks, where trust stalls, where retention gets decided, and what I would test next as a PM.

The question behind every teardown: where is the real product leverage hiding?

Sometimes it is onboarding. Sometimes it is a broken incentive. Sometimes it is a discoverability problem disguised as weak demand. The research below is my attempt to surface the product problem underneath the headline, then outline the metric, test, and risk that deserve PM attention.
